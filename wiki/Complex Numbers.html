Number Sets

  • Natural numbers
    • Set of all positive integers, including 0
    • Symbol: \(\mathbb{N}\)
  • Integers
    • All negative and positive integers, including 0
    • Contains all natural numbers
    • Symbol: \(\mathbb{Z}\)
  • Rational numbers
    • All numbers representable as a fraction
    • Contains all integers
    • Symbol: \(\mathbb{Q}\) (meaning quotient)
  • Irrational numbers
    • All numbers not representable as a fraction (eg. \(\pi\) and \(e\))
  • Real numbers
    • All rational and irrational numbers
    • Contains all integers, rational, and irrational numbers
    • Symbol: \(\mathbb{R}\)
  • Complex numbers
    • All numbers with a real and imaginary component
    • Contains all real numbers (imaginary component is 0)
    • Symbol: \(\mathbb{C}\)

Complex Numbers

i

Definition

  • \(i^2 = -1\)
  • \(i = \sqrt{-1}\)

Exponents

  • ...
  • \(i^{-2} = -1\)
  • \(i^{-1} = -i\)
  • \(i^0 = 1\)
  • \(i^1 = i\)
  • \(i^2 = -1\)
  • \(i^3 = -i\)
  • \(i^4 = 1\)
  • ...
  • Cycles every 4th power

Complex Number

Definition

  • A number with real and imaginary components
  • Written as \(a + bi\)
    • \(a\) and \(b\) are real (ie. \(a, b \in \mathbb{R}\))
    • Called rectangular or cartesian form

Components

  • For a complex number \(z = a + bi\)
  • Real part written as \(Re(z)\)
    • \(Re(z) = a\)
  • Imaginary part written as \(Im(z)\)
    • \(Im(z) = b\)

Complex Pair

  • For the complex number \(a + bi\)
  • Can also be expressed as \((a, b)\)
  • Or \((r, \theta)\) for polar form

Conjugate

Definition

  • For the complex number \(z = a + bi\)
  • The complex conjugate is \(\overline{z} = a - bi\)
  • Invert the sign of the imaginary part
  • Symbol: \(\overline{z}\)

i

  • If \(z = bi = 0 + bi\), \(\overline{z} = -bi\)
  • If \(z = a = a + 0i\), \(\overline{z} = a\)

Properties

  • \(\overline{z_1 \pm z_2} = \overline{z_1} \pm \overline{z_2}\)
  • \(\overline{z_1 \times z_2} = \overline{z_1} \times \overline{z_2}\)
  • \(\overline{\frac{z_1}{z_2}} = \frac{\overline{z_1}}{\overline{z_2}}\)

Arithmetic

Surds

$$ \begin{align*} \sqrt{-64} & = \sqrt{64 \times -1} \\ & = \sqrt{64} \times \sqrt{-1} \\ & = 8i \\ \\ \sqrt{-8} & = \sqrt{8} \times \sqrt{-1} \\ & = 2\sqrt{2}i \\ \end{align*} $$

  • Take out \(i\) from surd
  • Simplify surd

Addition and Subtraction

$$ \begin{align*} (a + bi) + (c + di) & = (a + c) + (b + d)i \\ (a + bi) - (c + di) & = (a - c) + (b - d)i \\ \end{align*} $$

  • Add or subtract real and imaginary components separately
  • Factor out \(i\) from imaginary components

Multiplication

Real Number

  • Multiply real and imaginary parts separately
  • \(k(a + bi) = ka + kbi, k \in \mathbb{R}\)

Complex Number

  • Multiply out as if it was a quadratic
  • \((a + bi)(c + di) = ac + adi + bci + bdi^2 = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i\)

Complex Conjugate

  • Results in difference of perfect squares, but replacing the subtraction with an addition
  • \((a + bi)(a - bi) = a^2 - abi + abi - bi^2 = a^2 + b^2\)

Division

Real Number

  • Divide real and imaginary parts separately
  • \(\frac{a + bi}{k} = \frac{a}{k} + \frac{b}{k}i\)

Complex Number

  • Rationalise the denominator by multiplying by the denominator's conjugate
  • \(\frac{a + bi}{c + di} \times \frac{c - di}{c - di}\)
  • Example:

$$ \begin{align*} \frac{2 - 3i}{1 + 2i} & = \frac{2 - 3i}{1 + 2i} \times \frac{1 - 2i}{1 - 2i} \\ & = \frac{2 - 4i - 3i - 6}{5} \\ & = \frac{-4 - 7i}{5} \\ \end{align*} $$

Equality

  • Equal iff real and imaginary parts are equal

Quadratics

  • Complex roots exist if discriminant is less than 0
  • Complex roots for quadratics always appear in conjugate pairs
  • Solve using quadratic formula or by completing the square

Formula from Roots

  • Given two roots \(a + bi\) and \(c + di\) for a quadratic
  • Find formula by expanding \((x - (a + bi))(x - (c + di))\)

Roots and Formula Relationship

Monic

  • Given a quadratic \(x^2 + bx + c\) with roots \(x_1\) and \(x_2\)
  • \(x_1 + x_2 = -b\)
  • \(x_1 x_2 = c\)

Non-Monic

  • Given a quadratic \(ax^2 + bx + c\) with roots \(x_1\) and \(x_2\)
  • \(x_1 + x_2 = -\frac{b}{a}\)
  • \(x_1 x_2 = \frac{c}{a}\)

Argand Diagram

  • 2D number line
  • Real axis on horizontal
  • Imaginary axis on vertical
  • Complex numbers represented as vectors starting at the origin

Modulus

$$ |a + bi| = \sqrt{a2 + b2} $$
  • Magnitude of complex number
  • Written \(|z|\) or \(\mod{z}\)

Argument

$$ \arg{a + bi} = \tan^{-1}{\frac{b}{a}} $$

  • Angle made with positive real axis
  • Written \(\arg{z}\)
  • Usually given symbol \(\theta\)

Principle Argument

  • \(-\pi < \theta \leq \pi\)

Operations

Multiplication

  • Add arguments
  • Multiply moduli

Multiplication by i

  • Rotation 90 degrees anti-clockwise
  • Exponents represent rotations that are multiples of 90 degrees
    • \(i^{-1}\) is 90 degrees clockwise
    • \(i^2\) is 180 degrees

Division

  • Subtract arguments
  • Divide moduli

Conjugate

  • Reflection about real axis (x axis)

Polar Form

  • Represent complex number with modulus (magnitude) and angle made with the positive real axis
  • \(z = r(\cos{\theta} + i \sin{\theta})\)
    • \(r \in \mathbb{R}, r \geq 0\)
    • \(\theta\) in radians, \(-\pi < \theta \leq \theta\)
  • Also written \(z = r \cis{\theta}\)

Simplifying

  • If \(r\) is negative:
    • Add or subtract \(\pi\) and make \(r\) positive
    • \(-2 \cis{\frac{\pi}{2}} = 2 \cis{-\frac{3\pi}{2}}\)
    • \(-3 \cis{\frac{5\pi}{6}} = 3 \cis{-\frac{\pi}{6}}\)

Arithmetic

Addition and Subtraction

  • Convert to cartesian form
  • Add normally
  • Convert back

Multiplication

  • Add arguments
  • Multiply moduli
  • \(r_1 \cis{\theta_1} r_2 \cis{\theta_2} = r_1 r_2 \cis{\theta_1 + \theta_2}\)

Division

  • Subtract arguments
  • Divide moduli
  • \(\frac{r_1 \cis{\theta_1}}{r_2 \cis{\theta_2}} = \frac{r_1}{r_2} \cis{\theta_1 - \theta_2}\)

Conjugate

  • Negate argument
  • \(\overline{r \cis{\theta}} = r \cis{-theta}\)

Regions in Complex Plane

Lines Perpendicular to Axes

Real Axis

  • \(Re(z) = k\)
  • Line perpendicular to real axis at \(k\)

Imaginary Axis

  • \(Im(z) = k\)
  • Line perpendicular to imaginary axis at \(k\)

Circles

  • \(|z| = k\)
    • Circle of radius \(k\) centred at origin
  • \(|z - (a + bi)| = k\)
    • Circle of radius \(k\) with centre \((a, b)\)

Perpendicular Bisectors

  • \(|z_1 - (a + bi)| = |z_2 - (c + di)|\)
  • Perpendicular bisector between points \((a, b)\) and \((c, d)\)

Rays

  • \(\arg{z} = \theta\)
  • Ray extending from origin in direction \(\theta\)
  • Excludes origin as \(\arg{z}\) is undefined for 0

De Moivre's Theorem

  • \((r \cis{\theta})^n = r^n \cis{n \theta}\)

Expansion of Trigonometric Functions

Roots of Real Number

  • For \(z^n = k\)
  • Has \(n\) complex solutions
  • One solution will be \(z = k\)
  • Are all evenly spaced around a unit circle
  • Divide unit circle into \(n\) equal parts
  • All have modulus \(k\)
  • Appear in conjugate pairs

Example

  • Find solutions to \(z^5 = 1\)
  • One solution is \(z = 1\)
  • Will divide unit circle into 5 equal parts
  • In \(\cis\) form:
    • \(z = 1 \cis{0}\)
    • \(z = 1 \cis{\frac{2\pi}{5}}\)
    • \(z = 1 \cis{\frac{4\pi}{5}}\)
    • \(z = 1 \cis{-\frac{4\pi}{5}}\)
    • \(z = 1 \cis{-\frac{2\pi}{5}}\)

Roots of Complex Number

Given a Root

  • Given one root of equation \(z^n = a + bi\)
  • Will be \(n\) roots
  • Other roots have same modulus as given root
  • Other roots will split unit circle into \(n\) equal parts
  • Angle between them will be \(\frac{2\pi}{n}\)

Example

  • Find \(z^6 = -8i\) given one solution is \(1 + i\)
    • Implies \((1 + i)^6 = -8i\)
  • Will divide unit circle into 6 equal parts
  • Angle between will be \(\frac{\pi}{3}\)
  • Solutions are (in \(\cis\) form):
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{\pi}{4}}\) (given)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{7\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{11\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{15\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{19\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{23\pi}{12}}\)
  • Since \(-\pi < \theta \leq \pi\), solutions are:
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{\pi}{4}}\) (given)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{7\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{\frac{11\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{-\frac{3\pi}{4}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{-\frac{5\pi}{12}}\)
    • \(\sqrt{2} \cis{-\frac{\pi}{12}}\)

Given No Roots

  • Given equation \(z^n = a + bi\)
  • Write \(a + bi\) as \(r \cis{\theta + 2k\pi}, k \in \mathbb{R}\)
  • Therefore solutions are \(\sqrt[n]{r \cis{\theta + 2k\pi}}\)
    • \(= \sqrt[n]{r} \cis{\frac{\theta}{n} + \frac{2k\pi}{n}}\)
  • Simplify
  • Substitute values of \(k\) from 0 through \(n - 1\) to find first \(n\) roots

Example

  • Find the cube roots of \(4\sqrt{3} + 4i\)
  • In \(\cis\) form: \(8 \cis(\frac{\pi}{6} + 2k\pi), k \in \mathbb{R}\)
  • Require \(z^3 = 8 \cis(\frac{\pi}{6} + 2k\pi)\):

$$ \begin{align*} z & = (8 \cis(\frac{\pi}{6} + 2k\pi))^{\frac{1}{3}} \\ & = 2 \cis(\frac{\pi}{18} + \frac{2k\pi}{3}) \\ \end{align*} $$

  • For first 3 values of \(k\):
    • \(k = 0\), \(z = 2 \cis{\frac{\pi}{18}}\)
    • \(k = 1\), \(z = 2 \cis{\frac{13\pi}{18}}\)
    • \(k = 2\), \(z = 2 \cis{-\frac{11\pi}{18}}\)
  • For higher values of \(k\), we end up with the same result as earlier roots

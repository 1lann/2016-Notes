Stoichiometry

Universal Gas Law

\[ pV = nRT \]
  • Where:
    • \(p\) is pressure
    • \(V\) is volume
    • \(n\) is moles of gas
    • \(R\) is the universal gas constant (8.314)
    • \(T\) is the temperature in Kelvin

Kelvin

\[ T_{\mbox{K}} = T_{^{\circ}\mbox{C}} + 273.15 \]
  • Measure of temperature
  • Symbol: \(\mbox{K}\)
  • Scale step size is same as Celsius scale
  • \(0\mbox{K}\) is absolute zero
    • Where particles in the system have no kinetic energy
  • \(0^{\circ}\mbox{C} = 273.15\mbox{K}\)

Percentage by Mass

  • Calculate molar mass of compound
  • Calculate molar mass of component of compound in the compound's molecular formula
  • Divide second value by first
  • Example:
    • Percentage by mass of \(O\) in \(CO2\): \(\frac{2 \times 16}{12.01 + 2 \times 16)}\)

Percentage Yield

\[ \mbox{yield} = \frac{\mbox{actual yield}}{\mbox{theoretical yield}} \times 100\% \]
  • Actual yield divided by theoretical yield

Empirical Formula

  • Find equation
  • For each compound other than oxygen (for a combustion analysis):
    • Calculate moles of compound produced
    • Calculate moles of the element inside the unknown compound we care about
    • Calculate mass of element
    • Convert to percentage by mass of element in unknown compound
  • Find mass of oxygen by subtracting all other masses from the original mass of the unknown compound
    • Convert to percentage by mass of oxygen in unknown compound
  • Create table of each element
    • Add in percentage by mass (ie. mass in 100g) of each element
    • Convert to moles by dividing by molar mass
    • Divide by the smallest number to convert to a ratio
    • Multiply all the ratios make them whole numbers (if there are any fractional ones)
  • Use ratios to write formula

Hydrous Salts

  • Where water is a component of the salt, held in a fixed stoichiometric ratio with the salt
  • To find empirical formula of hydrous salt (including ratios of anhydrous component and water):
    • Find empirical formula of anhydrous component (as above)
    • Find mass of water and mass of anhydrous compound
    • Divide by respective molar masses
    • Divide by smallest ratio
    • Write formula

Limiting Reactant

  • Find equation
  • Find moles of each reactant
  • Pick a reactant:
    • Using stoichiometric ratios defined in equation, find moles of other reactant required to fully react with the selected reactant
    • If this is less than what we have, then the selected reactant is limiting
    • If this is greater than what we have, then the other reactant is limiting
  • To calculate the amount of a product produced:
    • Find moles of limiting reactant
    • Multiply by stoichiometric ratios in equation to find moles of required product
    • Convert to mass
  • To calculate amount of excess reactant left over:
    • Find moles of excess reactant that react by multiplying limiting reactant by stoichiometric ratios in equation
    • Subtract from moles of excess reactant we have
    • Convert to mass

Gases

  • For a gas reaction at constant pressure, temperature
  • No need to convert to moles
  • Can use gas volumes with stoichiometric ratios

Efficiency Reactions

  • To find moles of a final product produced
    • Find moles of given reactant
    • Multiply by stoichiometric ratios and efficiencies through each stage
    • Find moles of product
    • Convert to mass

Improving Efficiency

  • For products in later stages that do not contribute to final result
    • Reuse them in earlier stages if they also act as a reactant somewhere

Things to Remember

  • Empirical formulas of hydrous salts must include the water's ratio
  • Ensure you use every piece of information from the question

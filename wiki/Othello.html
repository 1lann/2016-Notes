Act 2

Summary

Scene 1

  • Turkish fleet ruined in the storms around Cyprus
  • Cassio arrives on shore
  • Followed soon by Iago and Desdemona
  • Iago jokes about women's uselessness, how they are destined to be housewives
  • Othello and Desdemona head off together to the citadel
  • Iago tells Roderigo how much Cassio and Desdemona lust after each other
  • Makes plans for Roderigo to be in charge on night duty that night, tells him to anger Cassio somehow

Scene 2

  • Othello invites many people to a party to celebrate his marriage and the failure of the Turks

Scene 3

  • Othello goes to sleep early with Desdemona to spend time with her
  • Iago and Cassio on night watch
  • Iago gets Cassio drunk
  • Cassio and Roderigo go on night watch
  • Roderigo angers Cassio, they both come back inside
  • Montano provokes Cassio further
  • Iago watches as Cassio stabs Montano (who Iago was talking to)
  • Roderigo sounds a bell to alert the town and Othello
  • Othello comes into the room to see Montano bleeding
  • Cassio cannot speak out of shame for what he's done
  • Montano says he cannot speak as it hurts too much
  • Iago is left to explain what happened
    • Makes it appear as if he's restraining himself so he appears respectful and honest, wanting to protect Cassio as a friend
  • Othello strips Cassio of his title
  • Desdemona is woken, Othello is pissed that this woke his wife
  • Montano is lead off to get his wounds attended
  • Desdemona and Othello go back to bed
  • Cassio complains to Iago about his hurt reputation
  • Iago suggests he appeals to Othello to get his job back
  • Cassio refuses, says it won't work
  • Iago suggests going through Desdemona, appealing to her to get his job back
    • This is so Iago can make it look like Cassio and her are having an affair to Othello

Export and Import Price Index

Acronyms

  • XPI is export price index
  • MPI is import price index

Definition

  • Weighted average of the prices of a representative group of exports or imports for a country in a particular year

Calculation

  • Select a base year
  • Calculate weighted average price of exports/imports for the base year
  • Assign this a value of 100
  • Calculate weighted average price for subsequent years
  • Divide weighted average price for subsequent years by the base year's and multiply by 100

Significance

  • If the XPI or MPI is greater than 100
    • Weighted average price of imports/exports is higher than that of the base year
  • If the XPI or MPI is less than 100
    • Weighted average price of imports/exports is less than that of the base year
  • Don't care about actual number
    • Care about change in number from year to year

Control

  • Australia's XPI and MPI set by world market
  • Australia itself has little direct influence on its XPI and MPI

Recent Trends

Export Price Index

  • Decline since 2012
  • Caused by end of commodities boom and slowing growth in China

Import Price Index

  • Rising since 2011
  • Caused by depreciating dollar

Terms of Trade

Definition

  • Index that measures the relative movements in the prices of exports and imports
  • Measure of price only
    • No indication of volume or value

Meaning

  • Measures quantity of imports a country can purchase from a given quantity of exports
  • Example:
    • Given we exported 1 car worth $1 million last year
    • We can buy $1 million worth of imports
    • With a change in price, the quantity of imports we can buy will change

Formula

\[ \frac{\mbox{\mbox{XPI}}{\mbox{MPI}}} \times 100 \]
  • Export price index divided by import price index times 100

Derivation

  • The value of exports sold is equal to the value of imports purchased:
\[ \begin{align*} \mbox{XPI} \times \mbox{Export Quantity} & = \mbox{MPI} \times \mbox{Import Quantity} \\ \frac{\mbox{XPI}}{\mbox{MPI}} \times \mbox{Export Quantity} & = \mbox{Import Quantity} \\ \end{align*} \]

Movements in Terms of Trade

  • Don't care about actual value of terms of trade
  • Care about movements in the value
  • Writing for an unfavourable movement
    • Decline in TOT since 2012

Terminology

  • Favourable movement
    • Increase in terms of trade relative to a previous year
  • Unfavourable movement
    • Decrease in terms of trade relative to a previous year

Favourable Movement

  • Where the XPI rises relative to the MPI
  • Scenarios:
    • XPI rising, MPI falling
    • XPI rising, MPI doesn't move
    • XPI doesn't move, MPI falling
    • XPI rising, MPI rising slower
    • XPI falling, MPI falling faster

Unfavourable Movement

  • Where the XPI falls relative to the MPI
  • Scenarios:
    • XPI falling, MPI rising
    • XPI falling, MPI doesn't move
    • XPI doesn't move, MPI rising
    • XPI rising, MPI rising faster
    • XPI falling, MPI falling slower

Elasticity of Demand

  • Significance of a country's terms of trade to its economy depends on the price elasticity of demand for exports and imports

Inelastic Demand

  • Like Australia
  • Unfavourable movement
    • Since demand in inelastic
    • Little change in quantity of exports bought
    • Decreases export income
    • Unfavourable outcome for economy

Elastic Demand

  • Unfavourable movement
    • Increase in quantity of exports bought due to lower price
    • May increase total value of exports sold
    • Increase in export income
    • Favourable outcome for economy

Factors Affecting

XPI

World Growth

  • Decreased growth from major trading partners (China)
    • Forecast for China's growth in 2016 is 6-7%, average over last decade is 10%
    • Decreases demand for our exports
    • Decreases their price
      • Price for iron ore went from around $160 a tonne at the height of the mining boom to $40 a tonne recently
    • Decreases XPI
    • Causes unfavourable movement in TOT
  • End of commodities price boom
    • Price of iron ore dropping from 160 per tonne at height of boom to 40 a tonne recently
  • Productive capacity
    • Due to the commodities price boom
    • Large increase in production capacity of commodities across the world
      • Companies capitalising on high prices, aiming to increase profits
      • Eg. Brazil and Chile
    • Increases supply of commodities in the market
      • "Glut" in the market
      • Causes surplus of commodities
    • Surplus is cleared through price mechanisms
      • Decreases price
    • Decreases XPI
    • Compounds unfavourable movement

Diversification of Exports

  • Australia has low diversity in its exports
    • We export predominantly raw minerals and agricultural products
    • Our top three exports are iron ore, coal, and natural gas
      • Account for 37% of all exports
    • A consequence of a decade long mining boom
  • Decrease in the price of commodities has an amplified effect on the unfavourable movement in our TOT
    • Because of our reliance on these exports

MPI

Lack of Falling Prices in Manufactures

  • 15 - 20 years ago, the emergence of China as a manufacturing powerhouse in the world
    • Lead to large decline in the price of manufactured products
    • Decreased our MPI
  • No longer falling as rapidly as they once were
    • No longer a new phenomenon in the Australian market
  • Has remained fairly flat in the last few years

Depreciation in AUD

  • Depreciation in AUD since 2012
    • Increases price of imports in Australian dollar terms
    • Increases MPI
    • Causes unfavourable movement in TOT

Effects

Balance of Goods and Services

  • An unfavourable movement may decrease the price of our exports
  • Due to the inelastic nature of Australia's exports
    • Few substitutes due to the composition of our exports
    • Can't substitute raw minerals like iron ore
  • Decreases export income
  • Unfavourable movement also may increase the price of our imports
    • Causes a rise in the value of import spending
  • BOGS moves towards a deficit

Current Account Deficit

  • No direct link between TOT and incomes category
  • Since an unfavourable TOT movement causes an increase in BOGS deficit
    • Causes increase in CAD

Exchange Rate

  • Decrease in XPI
    • Decrease in export income due to inelasticity of our exports
    • Decreases demand for AUD
    • Causes depreciation in exchange rate
  • Increase in MPI
    • Increases import spending
    • Increases supply of AUD in market
    • Compounds depreciation

Inflation

  • Falling export income (above) causes falling aggregate expenditure
    • Decreases demand pull inflation
    • Decreases inflation rate
  • However, if the MPI increases
    • Increases costs for domestic businesses who must import intermediate goods during the production of consumption goods
    • Increases cost push inflation
    • Increases inflation rate
    • This could be compounded by a depreciating dollar (caused by unfavourable TOT movement)
      • Further increases import prices and cost push inflation

Domestic Economic Growth

  • XPI decrease
    • Decreases export income (above)
    • Decreases aggregate expenditure
  • MPI increase
    • Increases import spending
    • Decreases aggregate expenditure
  • Decreased aggregate expenditure causes slowing economic growth
    • This may be offset slightly by the competitive advantage bestowed upon import competing industries by the increased MPI

Unemployment

  • Reduced economic growth results in an increase in cyclical unemployment
    • Fall in derived demand for labour
    • May be offset slightly by a likely increase in employment from import competing industries and industries that benefit from the depreciation of the AUD (tourism and education)
  • Structural unemployment could occur in industries the falling XPI has severely affected

Purchasing Power

  • Unfavourable movement
    • Decreases quantity of imports a country can buy from a given volume of exports
    • Decreases the real purchasing power of a country's people
    • Decreases living standards

International Competitiveness

  • Unfavourable movement
    • Decrease in XPI decreases price of exports
    • Bestows competitive advantage on Australia
    • Increases our international competitiveness

Trade Balance

  • A favourable movement in the TOT should cause an increase in export income
    • Due to the inelastic nature of Australia's exports
  • A direct, positive, relationship between the TOT and BOGS

Negative Relationship

  • There may be circumstances where this relationship doesn't hold
    • Where there is an increase in XPI but a dramatic decrease in volume
    • Causes a decrease in value, and hence the BOGS
    • Rather than an increase in BOGS
  • Natural disaster
    • Severe reduction in export volume due to destruction of materials
    • Since price of exports is determined in the global market
      • Australia has little individual influence over the price
    • Price could still rise (favourable movement)
  • Export capacity
    • Price for exports is high due to high demand
      • Since price is determined in the world market
    • But there is a cap on the capacity of exports we can produce
    • If import prices are low at the same time
    • We may import a lot more than we export (trade deficit) while still having a favourable terms of trade movement

Recent Trends

Mining Boom

  • 2001 - 2011
  • Terms of trade index rose from 52 to 107
  • Favourable movement
  • Large increase in commodity prices

Causes

  • Rapid growth of emerging markets like China and India
    • Led to large increase in demand for commodities
    • Increased world commodity prices
    • Increased Australia's XPI
  • China becoming world's largest manufacturer
    • Increased efficiency in producing manufactured goods
    • Fall in price of manufactured goods due to lower average costs
    • Since Australia imports a large quantity of manufactured goods
    • Fall in Australia's MPI

Global Financial Crisis

  • 2008 - 2009
  • Severe unfavourable movement
  • Recovered quickly
    • Favourable movements through 2010 - 2012
    • Due to China stimulating their economy after the GFC

Recent Decline

  • 2012 - 2016
  • Unfavourable movement
  • Fall in world commodity prices at end of mining boom
  • Compounded by slowing growth in China

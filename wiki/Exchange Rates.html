Exchange Rates

Definition

  • The price of one country's currency in terms of another country's currency

Foreign Exchange Market

  • Market where the currency of other countries is bought and sold

Measurements

Bilateral

Definition

  • The bilateral exchange rate measures the value of one country's currency in terms of another country's currency
  • Also called the cross exchange rate

Example

  • For example, AUD in terms of USD
    • 1 AUD = 0.90 USD
    • 1 USD = 0.71 British pounds

Quoting

  • Most commonly quoted exchange rate is the bilateral exchange rate against USD
  • There are two ways to quote a currency using the bilateral exchange rate:
    • In terms of a country's purchasing power
      • 1 AUD = 0.75 USD
    • In terms of a common currency
      • 1 USD = 1.33 AUD

Trade Weighted Index

Definition

  • The trade weighted index is a measure of movements in the Australian dollar against a weighted average of 20 other currencies
  • The currencies are weighted according to their importance to Australia's international trade flows

Advantages Over Bilateral Measure

  • TWI is more accurate and more significant than the bilateral exchange rate holistically
    • Though the bilateral rate is more significant for an individual travelling to South Africa, for example
  • As it is trade weighted, it relates to changes in performance of Australia's balance of payments over time

Formula

  • Price of good in foreign currency
    • Multiply price of good in domestic currency by the bilateral rate, quoted in terms of the domestic currency (ie. domestic currency is 1)
  • Price of good in domestic currency
    • Multiply price of good in foreign currency by the bilateral rate, quoted in terms of the foreign currency (ie. foreign currency is 1)

Example

  • Price in foreign currency
    • Given a good costs $60 AUD
    • The exchange rate is 1 AUD = 0.75 USD
    • The good costs \(60 \times 0.75 = 45\) USD
  • Price in domestic currency
    • Given a good costs $80 USD
    • The exchange rate is 1 AUD = 0.75 USD
    • The exchange rate in terms of the USD is 1 USD = \(\frac{1}{0.75}\) = 1.33 AUD
    • The good costs \(80 \times 1.33 = 106.4\) AUD

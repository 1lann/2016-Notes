Reactions

Definitions

  • System
    • All particles involved in the reaction
  • Surroundings
    • Everything else

Non-Reversible

$$ \ce{CaCO3 + HCl -> CaCl2 + H2O + CO2} $$

  • Reactants left of arrow
  • Products right of arrow
  • Forward reaction from left to right
  • Reverse reaction from right to left
  • Single arrow meaning reaction cannot be reversed
  • Reaction proceeds to completion
    • All reactants converted to products

Reversible

$$ \ce{2NO2 <=> N2O4} $$

  • Double arrow meaning reaction is reversible
  • Never completes
  • Mixture of reactants and products present at all times

Ionic Equations

  • Split aqueous compounds into ions
  • Remove spectator ions (ones which remain aqueous on the right side of the equation)
  • Example:
    • \(\ce{2Na+ + SO4^{2-} + Ba^{2+} + 2NO3- -> 2Na+ + 2NO3- + BaSO4 (s)}\)
    • Without spectator ions: \(\ce{Ba^{2+} + SO4^{2-} -> BaSO4 (s)}\)

Acid Reactions

Acid Base

$$ \ce{H2SO4 (aq) + Ba(OH)2 -> BaSO4 (s) + 2H2O (l)} \\ \ce{H+ + OH- -> H2O (l)} \\ $$

  • Hydrogen ions and hydroxide ions
  • Form water

Acid Metal

$$ \ce{H2SO4 (aq) + Zn (s) -> ZnSO4 (aq) + H2 (g)} $$

  • Solid metal and acid
  • Form salt and hydrogen gas

Acid Carbonate

$$ \ce{H2SO4 (aq) + CaCO3 (s) -> CaSO4 (aq) + CO2 (g) + H2O (l)} $$

  • Carbonate and acid
  • Form salt, carbon dioxide, and water

Precipitation Reactions

$$ \ce{Ba{2+} + SO4{2-} -> BaSO4 (s)} $$

  • Two aqueous solutions
  • When combined, a combination of ions forms a solid
  • Causes the formation of a precipitate in the solution
